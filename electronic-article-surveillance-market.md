<p><p>Electronic Article Surveillance (EAS) is a security system primarily used in retail environments to prevent theft and reduce shrinkage. It employs a combination of tags and detection systems to monitor and secure merchandise. These tags, which can be either hard or soft, are removed or deactivated at the point of sale. If a tagged item passes through a detection system at an exit without being properly deactivated, an alarm is triggered, alerting staff to potential theft.</p><p>The Electronic Article Surveillance Market is expected to grow at a CAGR of 4.2% during the forecast period. Key drivers of this growth include increasing retail theft, the expanding e-commerce sector, and advancements in EAS technology. Retailers are adopting integrated solutions that combine EAS with inventory management systems to enhance operational efficiency. Additionally, the demand for RFID-based EAS systems is on the rise, offering enhanced tracking and security features. Emerging trends involve the integration of artificial intelligence and machine learning to analyze theft patterns and improve response strategies. As the retail landscape evolves, EAS will continue to play a critical role in securing merchandise and mitigating losses.</p></p>

<p><strong>The Electronic Article Surveillance Market Analysis by types is segmented into:</strong></p>
<p><ul><li>Hard Tag</li><li>Soft Tag</li><li>Deactivator or Detacher</li><li>Detection System</li><li>Permanent Deactivation Tag</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The Electronic Article Surveillance (EAS) market encompasses several key components. Hard tags are durable security devices typically used on high-value items, designed to be removed at checkout. Soft tags are adhesive labels suitable for less valuable merchandise and are easily deactivated. Deactivators or detachers are tools used by retailers to remove or disable tags at the point of sale. Detection systems are employed at store exits to alert staff of unauthorized items, while permanent deactivation tags remain embedded in products yet signal removal for theft prevention.</p></p>
