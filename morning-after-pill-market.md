<p><p>The morning-after pill market primarily consists of Levonorgestrel and Ulipristal Acetate. Levonorgestrel is a progestin-based option that inhibits ovulation and is effective within 72 hours of unprotected intercourse. It is available over-the-counter in many regions. Ulipristal Acetate, a selective progesterone receptor modulator, offers a longer window of efficacy, up to 120 hours after intercourse. Both options provide crucial access to emergency contraception but may vary in availability, pricing, and public awareness depending on regional regulations and healthcare systems.</p></p>
