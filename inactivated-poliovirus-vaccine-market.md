<p><p>The inactivated poliovirus vaccine (IPV) market is primarily categorized into two types based on the cell lines used for vaccine production: Human Diploid Cell (HDC) and Monkey Kidney Cell (MKC) vaccines. HDC vaccines utilize human diploid cells for viral culture, offering improved safety and reduced risk of contamination. In contrast, MKC vaccines are derived from monkey kidney cells, historically used but facing scrutiny due to potential contamination risks. Both types aim to provide effective immunity against poliovirus, with varying manufacturing and regulatory considerations.</p></p>
