<p><p>Cephalosporin antibacterial drugs are categorized into four generations, each with distinct characteristics and uses. First-generation cephalosporins are effective against gram-positive bacteria and are commonly used for skin infections. Second-generation cephalosporins offer improved coverage against gram-negative bacteria and are often used in respiratory tract infections. Third-generation cephalosporins have enhanced efficacy against resistant strains and are used for severe infections. Fourth-generation cephalosporins provide broad-spectrum activity against both gram-positive and gram-negative bacteria, making them suitable for hospital-acquired infections.</p></p>
