<p><p>The chromatography packing market is divided into two primary types: inorganic and organic polymer chromatography packing. Inorganic chromatography packing typically utilizes materials like silica or alumina, offering high thermal stability and chemical resistance, making them suitable for various analytical applications. In contrast, organic polymer chromatography packing is made from synthetic polymers, providing flexibility and broader compatibility with diverse solvents. Both types play crucial roles in separating and analyzing compounds in fields such as pharmaceuticals, environmental testing, and biochemical research.</p></p>
