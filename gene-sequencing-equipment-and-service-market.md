<p><p>The gene sequencing equipment and service market encompasses various technologies, primarily categorized into first, second, third, and fourth-generation sequencing. First-generation sequencing, such as Sanger sequencing, offers high accuracy but is time-consuming and costly. Second-generation sequencing, or next-generation sequencing (NGS), allows for high-throughput analysis, enabling rapid sequencing of large DNA fragments. Third-generation sequencing provides real-time sequencing of longer DNA strands, enhancing accuracy and speed. Fourth-generation sequencing is in development, featuring advancements like nanopore technology for even faster and more efficient sequencing.</p></p>
