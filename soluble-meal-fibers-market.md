<p><p>The soluble meal fibers market includes several key types, each with unique properties and applications. Insulin fibers, derived from chicory root, promote gut health and regulate blood sugar levels. Polydextrose, a synthetic fiber, is used as a low-calorie bulking agent in food products. Pectin, sourced from fruits, acts as a gelling agent and supports digestive health. Other market fibers may include oat beta-glucans and psyllium, providing additional health benefits and catering to diverse dietary needs.</p></p>
